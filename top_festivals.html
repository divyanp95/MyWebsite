Festivals

Udupi Paryaya

It is the bi-annual celebration of the Ashta matthas (eight monasteries) of Udupi that signifies the transfer of responsibility from one Swamiji (head priest) to the other. But, this will be a disgrace to the holy culture and interesting story behind this one of its kind festival.

Ganesh Chaturthi

This is another festival which is observed in religious fervor. Worshipping the Elephant faced God brings a festive mood, all across the region.

Mahamasthakabhisheka

Mahamasthakabhisheka is an important Jain festival held once in every twelve years in the town of Karkala in Udupi District. The festival is held in veneration of a 42feet high statue of Bahubali. The anointing of this statue last took place in 2015.

Mooru Theru Utsava

Mooru Ratha or three chariot festival at Udupi is a special event. The three chariots are pulled together during festival of Makara Sankranthi.

Monthi Fest

Monthi Fest is a mojor Udupi Catholic festival, which takes place on 8th September, every year. This festival celebrates Nativity of the Blessed Vergin Mary and the Udupi Catholic community, involves blessing of Novem (New Crops).

Ramzaan

Ramzaan is the ninth month of Islamic calendar, and is observed by Muslims worldwide as a month of fasting to commemorate the first revelation of Quran to Muhammad according to Islamic belief.

Laksha Deepotsava

Laksha Deepotsava, marking the beginning of daily Utsavs at the Sri Krishna Temple after the rainy season, was held with pomp and gaiety here on November 1.

Christmas

Christmas is the annual festival commemorating the birth of Jesus Christ, observed on December 25th, as a religious and cultural celebration by the people around Udupi. It is predominantly performed by Christians, but others also join in the festival.

Deepavali

Deepavali is afestival celebrated by all Hindus, to commemorate Lord Rama and his wife Sita's return to Ayodhya after 14-year exile. It was a dark night when they first returned; hence his people lit their houses with little lamps (diyas), so that Rama and Sita could find their way.

Attur Feast

The whole year round the pilgrims irrespective of caste and creed, come to visit the Shrine. People believe that St. Lawrence indeed is a powerful intercessor before God. This annual feast will be held at the end of month Jnauary.

Krishna Janmashtami

Sri Krishna Janmashtami is celebrated most vibrantly at Udupi. As a part of tradition , earthen pots are filled with milk and milk products arehung on 17 makeshift high mast towers at different places. Temple employees dressed gollas break the pots using long sticks.

Holi

Marathi and Kunbi Communities of Udupi district celebrated Holi in a unique way, quite different from that is being celebrated in either North India or Maharashtra. Holi is one of the important festivals for the Marathis as well as the Kunbis.
